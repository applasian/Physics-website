What is the Standard Model?

To have an idea of the standard model, find an object near you and ask yourself: What is this made out of?
In the early 20th century, physicists believed that all matter, including you, are made up of protons, neutrons and electrons.
But then again, we ask the question: What are these protons, neutrons and electrons made up of?
It wasn't until 1964 when Murray Gell-Mann and Geroge Zweig solved this puzzling problem by putting forth the idea of quarks.
Certain combinations of quarks made up these particles but physicists soon realised quarks weren't the only fundamental particles there is.
In fact, they discovered so many that categorised the elementary particles into what we know: quarks and leptons.
Some particles are reponsible for forces that hold the universe together, others only exist for a blink of an eye.
These elementary particles are just mere ingredients of the universes but with the drive the four forces, can nature create the universe we live in.

Gravity

Let's emphasise on the gravititational force because it is a bit weird compared to the other forces. In fact, it is the weakst force
by far, around 1040 times weaker than the electromagnetic force! Despite it's feebleness, Einstein was curious about how gravity worked,
which after decades he was finally able to elegantly describe it with his famous theories of General Relativity and Special Relativity.
The crux is that the Standard Model has a problem incorporating itself with Einstein's Theory. It cannot explain gravity in terms of particles,
the best it can do it to hypothesise a particle called the graviton, which is yet to be discovered. So the SM is not complete so to say,

Electromagnetism

The reason you can read this is because of the discovery of electromagnetism. Electromagnetism is constituted of electricity and magnetism
which are responsible for the interactions between electrically charged particles. Like magnets, like charges repel (- and - or + and +) while opposite charges
attract (+ and -). By building on this concept, we can explain a shocking amount about nature for example, how thunderstorms work, Earth's magnetic field,
and intermolecular forces. We humans need it for our computers, compasses, and even our existence. What you might not know is that light is an electromagnetic wave,
which we all know what light is. James Clerk Maxwell made things easier for us by proposing for equations by which we call Maxwell's equations: Do not fear the equations! It'll be explained in detail here. In case you're wondering, the integral and point form are
techincally the same thing. Electromagnetism is summed up by these four elegant equations which also describes the nature of light.
